2. Solutions

Master List
\(M = \frac{W_B \times 1000}{M_B \times V(ml)} \quad | \quad m = \frac{W_B \times 1000}{M_B \times W_A(g)}\)
\(\chi_B = \frac{n_B}{n_A + n_B} \quad | \quad ppm = \frac{W_B}{W_{soln}} \times 10^6\)
Henry: \(P = K_H \cdot \chi\)
Raoult: \(P_{Total} = P_A^0\chi_A + P_B^0\chi_B\)
RLVP: \(\frac{P_A^0 - P_s}{P_A^0} = i \chi_B\)
\(\Delta T_b = i K_b m \quad | \quad \Delta T_f = i K_f m\)
\(\pi = i CRT \quad (R = 0.0821)\)

\(i = 1+(n-1)\alpha\) (Dissociation). For Association, \(i = 1+(1/n-1)\alpha\). Always include \(i\) in colligative formulas!

3. Electrochemistry

Numerical Bible
\(\kappa = \frac{1}{R} \cdot \frac{l}{A} \quad | \quad G^* = l/A\)
\(\Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{C}\)
\(E = E^0 - \frac{0.0591}{n} \log Q\)
\(\Delta G^0 = -nFE^0 = -2.303 RT \log K_c\)
\(w = \frac{E \cdot I \cdot t}{96500} \quad | \quad Q = nF\)

üí° \(1F = 96487 \text{ C mol}^{-1}\). Charge of 1 electron = \(1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}\).

Organic Reaction Center

Haloalkanes ‚Ä¢ Alcohols ‚Ä¢ Aldehydes ‚Ä¢ Amines

10. Haloalkanes

Subst.

‚Ä¢ SN1: \(3^o > 2^o > 1^o\) (Stability of Carbocation)
‚Ä¢ SN2: \(1^o > 2^o > 3^o\) (Steric hindrance)

Racemic mixture (50% d + 50% l) is optically inactive due to external compensation.

11. Alcohols & Phenols

Vital

Electron withdrawing groups (\(-NO_2, -CN\)) \(\uparrow\) acidity of phenols. Electron donating (\(-CH_3, -OCH_3\)) \(\downarrow\) acidity.

Bond angle in \(CH_3-O-CH_3\) is \(111.7^o\) (Greater than tetrahedral due to repulsion between two bulky alkyl groups).

12. Aldehydes & Acids

Mechanisms

Order: \(HCHO > CH_3CHO > CH_3COCH_3\). Why? Inductive (+I) and Steric effects.

Lower the \(pKa\), stronger the acid. Trichloroacetic acid > Dichloro > Monochloro > Acetic acid.

13. Amines Dash

‚Ä¢ Aqueous (Methyl): \(2^o > 1^o > 3^o > NH_3\)
‚Ä¢ Aqueous (Ethyl): \(2^o > 3^o > 1^o > NH_3\)

14. Biomolecules

Essential

‚Ä¢ Reducing Sugars: All monosaccharides + Lactose/Maltose.
‚Ä¢ Non-Reducing: Sucrose.
‚Ä¢ Glucose Reacts: HI (n-hexane), \(NH_2OH\) (Oxime), \(Br_2/H_2O\) (Gluconic acid).

At a specific pH (Isoelectric point), amino acids exist as dipolar ions called Zwitterions. Most are optically active except Glycine.

Insulin contains 51 amino acids. Hb contains Iron. Primary (\(1^o\)) structure is the sequence; Secondary (\(2^o\)) is \(\alpha\)-helix or \(\beta\)-pleated.
