Unit 1: Solutions

Numerical Heavy
\(M = \frac{n_B}{V(L)} \quad | \quad m = \frac{n_B}{W_A(kg)} \quad | \quad \chi_B = \frac{n_B}{n_A + n_B}\)
\(\Delta T_b = i K_b m \quad (K_b = \text{Ebullioscopic})\)
\(\Delta T_f = i K_f m \quad (K_f = \text{Cryoscopic})\)
\(\pi = i CRT \quad | \quad \frac{P^0-P}{P^0} = i \chi_B\)

üí° Van't Hoff (\(i\)): Check if solute is NaCl (\(i=2\)), \(Na_2SO_4\) (\(i=3\)), or Urea (\(i=1\)).

Unit 2: Electrochemistry

Weightage
\(E_{cell} = E^0_{cell} - \frac{0.0591}{n} \log Q\)
\(\Lambda_m^0 = \nu_+ \lambda_+^0 + \nu_- \lambda_-^0 \quad | \quad \Delta G^0 = -nFE^0\)
\(Q = It = nF\)

üí° \(1F = 96487 \text{ C mol}^{-1}\) (usually taken as 96500).

Unit 3: Chemical Kinetics

Speed
0 Order: \(k = \frac{[A]_0 - [A]}{t}\)
1st Order: \(k = \frac{2.303}{t} \log \frac{[A]_0}{[A]}\)
\(t_{1/2} (0^{th}) = \frac{[A]_0}{2k} \quad | \quad t_{1/2} (1^{st}) = \frac{0.693}{k}\)
\(\log \frac{k_2}{k_1} = \frac{E_a}{2.303 R} \left[ \frac{T_2 - T_1}{T_1 T_2} \right]\)

Unit 4: d & f Elements

Reasoning
\(\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)} \, \text{BM}\)

üí° \(n\) = number of unpaired electrons.

Steady decrease in atomic size due to poor shielding of 4f electrons. Why 5d series \(\approx\) 4d series size? Answer: Lanthanoid Contraction.

Mn shows maximum (+7). Sc shows only (+3). Zn shows only (+2).

Unit 6 & 7: RX & ROH

Essential

SN1: \(3^o > 2^o > 1^o\) (Polar Protic Solvent).
SN2: \(1^o > 2^o > 3^o\) (Polar Aprotic Solvent).

Phenols: \(o/p\)-Nitrophenol > Phenol > Cresols. Nitrophenyl is very acidic due to -I and -R effect of nitro group.

Unit 8: Carbonyls & Acids

Heavy Weight

Aldol: \(\alpha\)-H must be present (e.g., Ethanol).
Cannizzaro: No \(\alpha\)-H (e.g., Benzaldehyde, Formaldehyde).

Aldehydes > Ketones for Nucleophilic Addition. (Steric & Inductive effects).

Unit 9: Amines Dash

Base Logic

Methyl (-CH3): \(2^o > 1^o > 3^o > NH_3\)
Ethyl (-C2H5): \(2^o > 3^o > 1^o > NH_3\)

Aniline is less basic than Ammonia due to delocalization of lone pair into benzene ring.
