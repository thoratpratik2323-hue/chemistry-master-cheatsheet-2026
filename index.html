7. p-Block Elements

Trends

F, O, N show unusual behavior due to small size, high electronegativity, and absence of d-orbitals.

Types: \(XX', XX'_3, XX'_5, XX'_7\). Always more reactive than pure halogens (except \(F_2\)).

\(XeF_2\) (Linear) | \(XeF_4\) (Square Planar) | \(XeF_6\) (Distorted Octahedral).

8. d & f Block Hub

Reasoning

Poor shielding of 4f electrons \(\rightarrow\) Increase in effective nuclear charge \(\rightarrow\) Size decrease. Consequences: Zr/Hf have same size.

Transition metals trap small atoms (H, C, N). Results: Non-stoichiometric, very hard, high melting point.

9. Coordination

Pure Logic

Primary Valency (Ionizable, ON) vs Secondary Valency (Non-ionizable, CN).

Linkage: Ambidentate ligands (SCN/NCS).
Ionization: Exchange of ions between inside/outside sphere.
